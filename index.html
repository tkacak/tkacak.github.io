About

I work on the methods researchers use to decide how many factors a set of items really measures — and on what happens to those decisions when the data are ordinal, incomplete, hierarchically structured, or full of cross-loadings.

Most of my work is Monte Carlo simulation: factor retention methods, exploratory graph analysis, factor extraction for ordinal indicators, and reliability coefficients under realistic violations of their assumptions. I also work with large-scale assessment data (PISA, PIRLS, TIMSS, ICILS), where I look at measurement invariance and item-format effects across countries.
